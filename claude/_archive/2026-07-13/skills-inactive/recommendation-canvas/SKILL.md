# Recommendation Canvas

A strategic proposal that articulates why an AI solution is worth building, what assumptions need validating, and how you will measure success. Not a PRD (no detailed requirements), not yet a business case (it informs one but needs validation first), not a feature list (outcomes, not capabilities). Especially suited to AI features, which carry higher uncertainty.

## The ten components

1. **Business outcome:** what is in it for the business? Format: direction, metric, outcome, context (e.g. "increase enterprise MRR 15% within 12 months").
2. **Product outcome:** what is in it for the customer?
3. **Problem statement:** persona-centric framing of the problem.
4. **Solution hypothesis:** an if/then bet with the experiments that would test it.
5. **Positioning statement:** value proposition and differentiation.
6. **Assumptions and unknowns:** what could invalidate this?
7. **PESTEL risks:** political, economic, social, technological, environmental, legal. (See [[pestel-analysis]].)
8. **Value justification:** why this is worth doing now.
9. **Success metrics:** SMART metrics tied to the outcomes above.
10. **What is next:** the strategic next steps.

## Why it works

- **Outcome-driven:** forces clarity on business *and* customer value.
- **Hypothesis-centric:** treats the solution as a bet to validate, not a commitment to defend.
- **Risk-explicit:** assumptions and risks are visible up front, which matters most for AI bets.
- **Exec-friendly:** comprehensive but structured for a C-level read.

## When

Use after initial discovery, when proposing an AI product or feature, pitching for budget or sponsorship, deciding whether an AI solution is worth pursuing, or aligning product, engineering, data science, and business. Do not use it for trivial tweaks, before any discovery, or as a substitute for experimentation. The canvas informs experiments; it does not replace them.
