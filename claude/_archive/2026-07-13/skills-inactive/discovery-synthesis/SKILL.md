# Discovery Synthesis

Turn raw notes into decisions. Work only from what the notes actually say; quote the evidence, do not extrapolate.

## Produce
1. **Themes** - cluster the raw observations. For each: the pattern, supporting quotes, and how many sources mentioned it (signal vs one loud voice).
2. **JTBD forces** for the key jobs: the **push** (what frustrates them today), the **pull** (what attracts the new way), the **anxiety** (what worries them about switching), and the **habit** (what keeps them on the status quo).
3. **Opportunity solution tree** (Teresa Torres): the desired **outcome** -> **opportunities** (unmet needs and pains, in the customer's own words) -> candidate **solutions** -> the **assumption tests** that would de-risk them.
4. **So what** - the one to three opportunities worth pursuing now, and why.

## Rules
- Opportunities are needs, not solutions. "Users cannot tell which invoices are overdue," not "add a red badge."
- Note frequency so a single vivid quote does not masquerade as a trend.
- Where the evidence is thin, flag it rather than inventing a theme.
