# Stakeholder Engagement Advisor

Generic engagement fails because it gives everyone the same message, medium, and cadence. An exec sponsor who privately disagrees needs different handling than an impacted frontline user who was never consulted. Run this once per priority stakeholder: anyone in "manage closely", every Q1 (high-impact, low-power) stakeholder from [[stakeholder-mapping]], and anyone who could block, delay, or reshape the initiative.

## Diagnose before you plan

Skipping straight to tactics produces a message crafted for the wrong motivation. Work two phases:

1. **Diagnosis:** who is this person, what drives them, what is the relationship history?
2. **Planning:** what will you say, through which medium, to what desired outcome?

Map their motivations separately from yours. Where they overlap you have an alignment path; where they diverge you have a negotiation to prepare for. Planning only your side is the most common failure.

## Three decision points

1. **Profile:** exec sponsor / cross-functional peer / end user or community rep / skeptic or blocker / newly identified.
2. **Power and impact:** how much authority they hold versus how much the outcome affects them (assess independently).
3. **Context:** first contact / pre-milestone alignment / resistance or conflict / elevating an under-represented voice / ongoing maintenance.

The combination drives the message, medium, and ask. A pre-milestone exec sponsor gets a pre-read plus a 1:1 to surface unstated concerns before the room does. A skeptic gets listening before persuading.

## Always end with a committed next action

Every plan names who does what, by when, through which medium, with what key message and desired outcome. Without that it is an intention, not a plan. The next action is the only part that moves the relationship.

## Edge cases

- **Large group or community:** engage through a validated proxy who can genuinely speak for them, not just whoever shows up to meetings. Verify representativeness, then build broader touchpoints over time.
- **History of conflict:** the first engagement's goal is to understand concerns, not to win. Lead with listening.
- **Implausible given time or access:** scope down to what is achievable and name the residual risk explicitly.
