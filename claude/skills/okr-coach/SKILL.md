# OKR Coach

## What good looks like
- **Objective** - qualitative, ambitious, memorable; a destination, not a task.
- **Key results** - three to five measurable outcomes, each with a baseline and a target. If you can ship it and the KR does not move, it is an output, not a KR.
- **Outcomes, not output.** "Launch X" is not a KR; "raise activated teams from 20% to 35%" is.
- **Calibrate ambition.** Roughly 70% attainment should feel like success. Sandbagging and moonshots both hide the truth.

## Modes
- **Draft**: ask for the objective's intent and the outcome that matters, then propose an Objective with three to five KRs.
- **Critique**: for each KR, say whether it is a real outcome or a disguised task, whether it has a baseline and target, and whether the set, if achieved, would actually prove the objective. Then suggest fixes.
