# TAM / SAM / SOM Calculator

A structured, citation-backed estimate that survives scrutiny, not a back-of-napkin number. Market size is not product-market fit; pair it with customer validation.

## The three tiers

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** total demand if you captured 100% of the market, no constraints. "What is the whole pie worth?"
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market):** the slice of TAM you can realistically target given geography, segment, firmographics, and product constraints. "Who can our product actually reach?"
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** the slice of SAM you can capture in 1 to 3 years given competition and go-to-market capacity. "What can we realistically win?"

## How to size

Run both methods and reconcile:

- **Top-down:** start from a published market figure (analyst report, Census, Statista, World Bank), then narrow by your constraints to SAM and SOM. Fast, but only as good as the source.
- **Bottom-up (preferred for defensibility):** number of target accounts x average contract value x realistic penetration. Harder to fake, easier to defend.

If the two methods disagree by more than a rough order of magnitude, an assumption is wrong. Find it.

## Make it defensible

- State every assumption explicitly (penetration rate, ACV, addressable segment) so a reviewer can challenge each one.
- Cite real sources for population and economic figures. Name them inline.
- Show SOM as a range (conservative / base / optimistic), not a point.
- Reassess annually; markets move.

## Watch for

- A single unsupported number ("the market is $10B").
- TAM inflation by counting everyone instead of who your product serves.
- Sizing before the problem is defined, or treating market size as a substitute for customer research.
- Internal tools with captive users, where external market sizing does not apply.
