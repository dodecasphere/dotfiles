# Stakeholder Mapping

Run two grids on your stakeholder list, then deliberately compare them. Each grid shows something the other cannot; the insight lives in the gap between them. Run this after you have a full stakeholder list and before planning per-person outreach (see [[stakeholder-engagement-advisor]]).

## The two grids

**Power x Interest** (the one most PMs know). How much can each stakeholder influence the initiative, and how much do they care?

- High power, high interest: **manage closely** (co-design, frequent touchpoints).
- High power, low interest: **keep satisfied** (exec briefings, strategic framing).
- Low power, high interest: **keep informed** (demos, transparency).
- Low power, low interest: **monitor** (light check-ins).

**Impact x Power.** Who bears the consequences of the outcome, and how much organizational power do they actually hold?

- High impact, high power: impacted and empowered, manage closely.
- High impact, low power (**Q1**): impacted but marginalized. Elevate deliberately.
- Low impact, high power: gatekeepers. Manage the relationship, lower engagement depth.
- Low impact, low power: monitor.

Impact and power are not the same axis. A frontline support agent has high impact (their workflow changes completely) but low power (no roadmap seat). A VP of Finance has high power (budget) but low impact (the product does not change their work). Conflating the two is the most common mapping error.

## The comparison is the point

Place both grids side by side and find the gaps:

- Who is "keep informed" on grid one but Q1 (high impact, low power) on grid two? You are under-engaging someone the product hits hardest. That is a product risk, not just an equity issue: Q1 people experience your failure modes first and appear in your feedback loops last.
- Who is "manage closely" on grid one but low impact on grid two? Watch for over-investing in powerful people with little stake.

## Then plan migration

For stakeholders you need to move, name the target quadrant, the action that triggers the move, and who owns it. Q1 to empowered means a named role in research, usability testing, and requirements review, not just an open door (an open door only serves people who already know they have a seat). Skeptic to ally means identifying what they need to believe, then designing 1:1 engagement to address it.

Feed every "manage closely" and every Q1 stakeholder into [[stakeholder-engagement-advisor]] for a tactical plan.
