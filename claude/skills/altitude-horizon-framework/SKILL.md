# Altitude and Horizon Framework

The two axes that shift in the PM-to-Director transition. Not a seniority hierarchy: a PM at the right altitude for their role is doing excellent work; a Director stuck at PM altitude is leaving their actual job undone.

## The two axes

**Altitude (scope).** PM: close to the ground (customer problems, individual features, sprint priorities, one team). Director: the portfolio, cross-functional systems, org dynamics, budget allocation, market positioning. The shift is not losing customer empathy; it is zooming out to see the whole restaurant, not one table.

**Horizon (time).** PM: days, weeks, sprints, a quarter at most. Director: a quarter as the floor, annual cycles, multi-year strategy. Directors decide where the product ecosystem needs to be in a year, then work backward.

## The four transition zones

Most people struggle with one or two. Diagnosing which is the leverage point.

1. **Thinking altitude.** Stop solving individual customer problems directly. Start designing systems and teams that solve classes of problems.
2. **Persona shift.** Stop obsessing over individual user personas. Start thinking in buyer personas, market cohorts, org stakeholders, and exec dynamics.
3. **Hero syndrome recovery.** Stop being the one who saves the day for the pat on the back. Start getting satisfaction from team success. Your product is your people, not the roadmap.
4. **Direction creation.** Stop waiting for clear direction from above. Start translating company strategy into team clarity even when the inputs are incomplete.

Named failure modes to watch: Hero Syndrome, Allergic to Process, People-Pleaser Leadership, Instant Gratification Trap.

## The Cascading Context Map

When direction from above is vague, Directors do not wait; they cascade. Six steps:

1. Listen to top-level strategy (QBRs, exec comms).
2. Extract 3 to 5 stated priorities, not 20 bullets.
3. Map layer two: how does our business unit deliver those?
4. Map layer three: how does our product portfolio deliver that?
5. Map layer four: what are my team's specific accountabilities that drive layer three?
6. Communicate the cascade, including *why* each piece connects upward.

This fixes teams "wandering in the wilderness" shipping work disconnected from strategy. The core principle: waiting for perfect clarity is a PM habit; creating imperfect-but-useful clarity downward is a Director skill.
