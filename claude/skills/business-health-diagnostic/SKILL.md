# Business Health Diagnostic

Health is the four dimensions read together and directionally (improving or degrading), benchmarked to your stage. A single green metric proves nothing. Pull numbers, score each dimension, rank the red flags by urgency, then recommend the top three actions.

## The four dimensions

1. **Growth and retention** - revenue growth rate, NRR, churn, Quick Ratio.
2. **Unit economics** - CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, payback, gross margin.
3. **Capital efficiency** - net burn, runway, Rule of 40, magic number.
4. **Strategic position** - pricing vs market, moat, revenue concentration, operating leverage.

Use [[finance-metrics-quickref]] for formulas and stage benchmarks.

## Gather context first

Stage (pre-$10M / $10-50M / $50M+ ARR), motion (PLG, sales-led, hybrid), and segment (SMB, mid-market, enterprise). Benchmarks shift by stage: early stage optimizes for growth, scale stage for efficiency. Ask for each metric; accept "unknown" rather than a guess.

## Triage red flags by urgency

**Critical (fix now):** runway <6 mo, LTV:CAC <1.5:1, churn accelerating cohort-over-cohort, NRR <90%, magic number <0.3.

**High (this quarter):** Rule of 40 <25, payback >24 mo, Quick Ratio <2, gross margin <60%, top-10 concentration >50%.

**Medium (within 6 months):** NRR 90-100% (flat), magic number 0.3-0.5, negative operating leverage, churn stable but high.

## Deliver

A health scorecard (Healthy / Moderate / Concerning / Critical), per-dimension scores, the ranked red flags, and three to five prioritized actions with expected impact and a metric target for each. Sequence fixes; do not try to move all four dimensions at once.

## Watch for

- Celebrating a single metric ("revenue up 50%!") while burn, churn, or unit economics rot.
- Stage-blind reading: early-stage burn is fine, scale-stage burn is a problem.
- Lagging indicators only. Churn and NRR confirm a problem late; track leading signals (activation, time-to-value, onboarding completion).
