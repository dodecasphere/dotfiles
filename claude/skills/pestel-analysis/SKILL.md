# PESTEL Analysis

Outward-facing assessment of the big-picture forces shaping your product's environment, beyond your control. Not competitive analysis (that is rivals) and not internal analysis (that is your strengths and weaknesses). PESTEL is the macro context around both.

## The six forces

1. **Political:** government policy, stability, trade rules, taxation.
2. **Economic:** growth, inflation, exchange rates, consumer spending, capital cost.
3. **Social:** demographics, cultural trends, lifestyle shifts, consumer attitudes.
4. **Technological:** advances, R&D, automation, digital transformation, AI.
5. **Environmental:** climate, sustainability, resource scarcity, related regulation.
6. **Legal:** compliance, IP, employment, privacy, health and safety.

(From Aguilar's 1967 PEST framework, extended to six.)

## How to run it

For each force, do not list generic facts. For every item answer two questions:

1. **Specific impact:** how could this concretely affect *this* product? (e.g. "EU AI Act requires explainable decisions, so our invoice automation must justify its recommendations.")
2. **Action:** can you adjust strategy because of it? If neither a threat nor an opportunity is actionable, drop it.

Then classify each item as opportunity or threat, rate likelihood and impact, and pull the few that are both likely and high-impact into the strategy or risk register.

## Use when

Entering a new market or geography, annual or 3-to-5-year planning, assessing viability in a shifting environment, or showing environmental awareness to execs and investors. Skip it for tactical short-term calls or when the external environment is stable and well understood.

## Watch for

- Generic lists with no product-specific impact or action.
- Treating it as a one-time exercise; the macro environment moves, so reassess.
- Using it as a substitute for customer research. PESTEL is macro, not micro.
