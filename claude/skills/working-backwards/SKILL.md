# Working Backwards (PR-FAQ)

Write the launch artifacts first, before any spec or code. If you cannot write a compelling, specific press release, the idea is not ready - and that is the point.

## Produce
1. **Press release (one page), written as if it already shipped.** Headline, subhead, dateline, the customer problem, the solution, a leader quote, a believable customer quote, and how to get started. Concrete and specific.
2. **Customer FAQ.** What a real customer or journalist would ask: what is it, what does it cost, how is it different, what are the limits, when can I get it.
3. **Internal FAQ (the hard one).** What your team and execs will ask: why now, why us, what has to be true, the riskiest assumption, what we are explicitly NOT doing, the unit economics, the dependencies, what could kill it.

## Rules
- Customer-first and outcome-first: describe the customer's better life, not a feature list.
- Specifics over adjectives. "Cuts reconciliation from 3 days to 20 minutes" beats "dramatically faster."
- The internal FAQ is where the idea lives or dies. Be honest about what must be true.
- If you are inventing facts to make the release sing, stop and name the assumption instead.

Offer to save the PR-FAQ as a Markdown file the user can open in Google Docs.
