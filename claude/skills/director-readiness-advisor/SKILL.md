# Director Readiness Advisor

Coaching that names what is actually hard about the PM-to-Director jump, why, and what to do. Not a readiness checklist. Built on [[altitude-horizon-framework]] (altitude, horizon, the four transition zones, the Waiter vs Restaurant Operator distinction, and the named failure modes).

## First, locate yourself

The transition looks different depending on where you are. The biggest mistake is applying advice from the wrong stage.

1. **Preparing:** still a PM, building toward the role.
2. **Interviewing:** active internal or external search.
3. **Newly landed:** first ~6 months as Director.
4. **Recalibrating:** been a Director a while and something is not clicking.

## If you are interviewing (your situation now)

Clarify the context (internal promotion, external search, or both), then focus the prep:

- **Internalize the framework, do not memorize it.** Work through [[altitude-horizon-framework]] as a study session. After each section, stop and answer: "Where have I operated at this altitude or horizon? What is my story here?" The four transition zones and named failure modes map almost directly to common Director interview questions. The most effective prep is making the thinking come out naturally, not reciting answers.
- **Build your stories.** Connect each zone to real experience. If you cannot point to a moment where you created direction from vague input, designed a system instead of solving one problem, or got satisfaction from team success over personal heroics, that is a gap to address honestly rather than paper over.
- **Practice out loud.** Rehearse answers using the framework's language. Interviewers probe for the same muscles the "preparing" exercises build; if you cannot do the exercise, you have not yet built the answer.
- **Name your gaps.** Identify which transition zones you have not yet demonstrated, and either find the story or be honest about the growth edge. Interviewers trust a candidate who knows their gap more than one who pretends not to have one.

## For the other stages

- **Preparing:** pick the one or two transition zones causing friction and build deliberate reps before the title changes.
- **Newly landed:** resist Hero Syndrome and the urge to keep doing the PM job well; your job is now the team and the system.
- **Recalibrating:** diagnose which failure mode crept in (Allergic to Process, People-Pleaser Leadership, Instant Gratification Trap) and correct at the altitude, not the symptom.
